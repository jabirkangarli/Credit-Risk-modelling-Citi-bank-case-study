1. Business Problem¶

Following the financial crisis of 2008, banks began to pay more attention to credit risk, which is believed to be among the root causes of the crisis.

Credit risk occurs when a borrower fails to make required payments on a debt. For financial institutions and other lenders, the risk is an inherent part of the lending paradigm. It is difficult to determine the level of risk associated with each loan. The probability of default, the amount of exposure at the time of default, how much the loan is expected to be worth at the time of default, and the overall loss if there is a default are all factors that go into the complex credit risk calculation.

Credit risk itself has the following main types:

  • Credit default risk

  • Concentration risk

  • Probability of Default (POD)

  • Loss Given Default (LGD)

  • Exposure at Default (EAD)

Credit institutions now have a plethora of models at their disposal for estimating credit risk and, thus, the likelihood of default. These models look for behavioral patterns from historical data to guess how a consumer will behave in the future.

Some of the models used for predicting binary outcomes such as default/non-default include logistic regression, decision trees, neural networks, and Naive Bayes classifier. However, despite their diversity and undeniable benefits to banking system stability, these models are not always adequate to deal with the complex circumstances that characterize many economies.

CITI, as one of the leading corporate banks in the world, puts a huge amount of effort and teamwork to develop models and have solid outputs of the developed models which assess their clients’ credit risk.

The aim of our case is to build a model which is assessing one-year probability of default of a company. We worked on two given datasets to develop a model which would give better results than the original one. The original dataset used for developing the original model contained obligors from North America region and their financials variables.

The dataset contains 22 columns with the following important variables:

  • EBIT - earnings before interest and taxes (revenue -expenses (excluding tax and interest)). It’s useful because it helps identify a company’s ability to generate enough earnings to be profitable, pay its own debt and fund ongoing operations

  • Growth in Leverage - refers to the use of debt (borrowed funds) to amplify returns from an investment or project. A highly leveraged company has more debt than equity

  • ROA (Return On Assets) - measure of how much profit a business generates from its capital. ROA = net income/ total assets. Higher ROA, more efficient the company is at generating profits

  • The cash ratio – A measurement of a company’s liquidity, specifically the ratio of a company’s total cash and cash equivalents to its current liabilities. It calculates the company’s ability to pay its short-term debt with cash or near-cash resources

  • Total debt to total assets – A leverage ratio that defines the total amount of debt relative to assets owned by a company. It can reflect how financially stable a company is. The higher the ratio, the higher the degree of leverage and consequently, the higher the risk of investing in that company

  • EBITDA to total Debt - DEBT/EBITDA (EBITDA- earnings before taxes, depreciation and amortization). It’s a ratio that measures the amount of income generated and available to pay down debt before covering interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization. A high ratio can indicate a company has a too-heavy debt load

  • Tangible net worth - determined by deducting intangible assets from the total net worth of a company. (Intangible assets include intellectual property rights such as patents, copyrights and company goodwill. These assets cannot be easily converted to cash and are less valuable to lenders.) Higher result is better

  • Free cash flow (FCF) - represents the cash a company generates after accounting for cash outflows to support operations and maintain its capital assets

  • Retained earnings - the amount of net income left over for the business after it has paid out dividends to its shareholders. Retained earnings could be used for funding an expansion or paying dividends to shareholders at a later date and etc

  • Tot.Liab. To Tangible Net Worth - Companies with low amounts of debt compared to their tangible net worth are considered financially healthier than firms with higher levels of debt.

  • Real Growth in Stock Equity - Stockholder's equity is the total value of assets owned by an investor after deducting and settling liabilities.

def building_logit_models(dev_dataset, logit_models, testing_dataset=None, 
                           table_of_models=None, addtional_analyses_columns=None):
    """
    Created on Mon Sep 22 12:24:57 2021
    
    @author: db66143
    
    """
    
    from sklearn.metrics import roc_curve
    from sklearn.metrics import roc_auc_score
    import statsmodels.api as sm
    from statsmodels.stats.outliers_influence import variance_inflation_factor
    import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
    import pandas as pd
    import numpy as np
    import os
    import sys
    
    # initilization of tabels and paths
    if table_of_models is None:
        table_of_models = pd.DataFrame(columns=['model', 'model_type', 'variables', 'number_of_var',
                                        'AR', 'AUC', 'params', 'p_vals', 'VIF', 'Wald_test_joined',
                                        '-2LOG L', 'AIC', 'BIC', 'HQIC'])
    if not os.path.isdir('models'):
        os.mkdir('models')
            
    ### fitting challengers
    for model_name, model_params in logit_models.items():

        ### Logit input preparation 
        # adding constant if not in dataset
        
        if 'const' not in model_params['exog']:
            print('adding const')
            dev_dataset = sm.add_constant(dev_dataset, prepend=False, has_constant='skip' )
            model_params['exog'] += ['const']
            if testing_dataset is not None:
                testing_dataset = sm.add_constant(testing_dataset, prepend=False, has_constant='skip' )

        # splitting params to dataset related and not
        target = model_params['endog']
        model_variables = model_params['exog']
        logit_model_input = {}
        for key, val in model_params.items():
            try:
                logit_model_input[key] = dev_dataset[val]
            except KeyError:
                logit_model_input[key] = val
                
        ### Logit      
        logit = sm.Logit(**logit_model_input)
        challenger_model = logit.fit()
                
        dev_dataset['temp_pred'] = challenger_model.predict()
        challenger_auc = roc_auc_score(dev_dataset[target], dev_dataset['temp_pred'])
        challenger_ar = 2*challenger_auc-1
        print(model_name, model_variables, round(challenger_ar,2))
        
        VIF = np.array([variance_inflation_factor(dev_dataset[model_variables].values, i)
                              for i in range(len(dev_dataset[model_variables].columns))])
    
        
        gather_params = {}
        gather_params['model'] = str(model_name)
        gather_params['variables'] = str(model_variables)
        gather_params['model_type'] = 'logit'
        gather_params['number_of_var'] = len(model_variables)
        gather_params['AR'] = challenger_ar
        gather_params['AUC'] = challenger_auc
        gather_params['params'] =str(dict(zip(model_variables , challenger_model.params.tolist())))
        gather_params['p_vals'] = str(dict(zip(model_variables , challenger_model.pvalues)))
        gather_params['VIF']  = str(dict(zip(model_variables , VIF)))  
        
        # Wald test joined significance
        # http://web.sgh.waw.pl/~jmuck/Ekonometria/EkonometriaPrezentacja2.pdf (slide 20)
        # r_matrix calculation (This tests that each coefficient is jointly statistically significantly different from zero.)
        # https://www.statsmodels.org/stable/generated/statsmodels.duration.hazard_regression.PHRegResults.f_test.html#statsmodels.duration.hazard_regression.PHRegResults.f_test (r_matrix)
        # Wald test in cox ph: 
        # https://www.statsmodels.org/stable/generated/statsmodels.duration.hazard_regression.PHRegResults.wald_test.html#statsmodels.duration.hazard_regression.PHRegResults.wald_test
        
        r_matrix = np.identity(len(challenger_model.params))[1:,:]
        gather_params['Wald_test_joined']  = challenger_model.wald_test(r_matrix).summary()
        
        # information criteria 
        
        llf = challenger_model.llf #log_likelihood_of_model
        gather_params['-2LOG L']  = (-2)*llf
        gather_params['AIC']  = sm.tools.eval_measures.aic(llf, nobs=len(dev_dataset), df_modelwc=len(challenger_model.params))
        gather_params['BIC']  = sm.tools.eval_measures.bic(llf, nobs=len(dev_dataset), df_modelwc=len(challenger_model.params))
        gather_params['HQIC']  = sm.tools.eval_measures.hqic(llf, nobs=len(dev_dataset), df_modelwc=len(challenger_model.params))
    
    
        # addtional_AR_analyses
        if addtional_analyses_columns is not None:
            for column in addtional_analyses_columns:
                gather_subgroup_params = {}
                for subgroup in dev_dataset[column].unique():
                    # Value error while no defaults for given subgroup
                    try: 
                       gather_subgroup_params[subgroup] = 2 * roc_auc_score(
                                    dev_dataset[dev_dataset[column]==subgroup][target], 
                                    dev_dataset[dev_dataset[column]==subgroup]['temp_pred'])-1 
                    except ValueError:
                        gather_subgroup_params[subgroup] = 'no defaults'
                                                
                gather_params['AR_per_'+column+'_devdataset'] = str(gather_subgroup_params)
     
                   
        #ROC chart
        fpr, tpr, thresholds = roc_curve(dev_dataset[target], challenger_model.predict())
        plt.figure()
        plt.plot(fpr, tpr, label='Accuracy Ratio = %0.4f' % challenger_ar)
        plt.plot([0, 1], [0, 1],'r--')
        plt.xlim([0.0, 1.0])
        plt.ylim([0.0, 1.05])
        plt.title(rf'{model_name} - Receiver operating characteristic curve (ROC)'.format(model_name))
        plt.xlabel('Percent of Sample Ordered by Score')
        plt.ylabel('Percent of Target')
        plt.legend(loc='lower right')
        plt.savefig(rf'models/{model_name} Receiver operating characteristic curve (ROC).png'.format(model_name),
                    bbox_inches='tight')
        plt.close()
            
    
        # testing set
        if testing_dataset is not None:
            testing_dataset['temp_pred'] = challenger_model.predict(exog=testing_dataset[model_variables])
            try:
                gather_params['AUC_testing_dataset'] = roc_auc_score(testing_dataset[target],
                                                                            testing_dataset['temp_pred'])
                gather_params['AR_testing_dataset'] = 2 * gather_params['AUC_testing_dataset'] - 1
            except ValueError:
                gather_params['AUC_testing_dataset'] = 'no defaults'
                gather_params['AR_testing_dataset'] = 'no defaults'
            
            # addtional_AR_analyses
            if addtional_analyses_columns is not None:
                for column in addtional_analyses_columns:
                    gather_subgroup_params = {}
                    for subgroup in testing_dataset[column].unique():
                        # Value error while no defaults for given subgroup
                        try: 
                           gather_subgroup_params[subgroup] = 2 * roc_auc_score(
                                        testing_dataset[testing_dataset[column]==subgroup][target], 
                                        testing_dataset[testing_dataset[column]==subgroup]['temp_pred'])-1 
                        except ValueError:
                            gather_subgroup_params[subgroup] = 'no defaults'
                                                    
                    gather_params['AR_per_'+column+'_testing_dataset'] = str(gather_subgroup_params)     
            testing_dataset.rename(columns={'temp_pred': model_name+'_logit_pred'}, inplace=True)
             
        table_of_models = table_of_models.append(gather_params, ignore_index=True) 
        dev_dataset.rename(columns={'temp_pred': model_name+'_logit_pred'}, inplace=True)
        
        
        # saving params to .txt file
        with open('models/'+model_name+'_model_summary.txt', 'w') as f:
            f.write('Model summary:')
            f.write(challenger_model.summary().as_text())
            f.write('\n\nWald test:')
            f.write(challenger_model.wald_test_terms().table.to_string())
            f.write('\n\n\n')
            for key, val in gather_params.items():
                f.write('\n{}:'.format(key))
                f.write('{}'.format(val))
                f.write('\n')
        
    return dev_dataset, testing_dataset, table_of_models

4. Building new models¶

4.1 Proposed models¶

The proposed models are the following ones:

  • Random Forest classifier is a machine learning algorithm that contains a number of decision trees on various subsets of the given dataset and takes the average to improve the predictive accuracy of that dataset. Instead of relying on one decision tree, the random forest takes the prediction from each tree and predicts the final output based on the majority vote of predictions. Its advantages are that it predicts output with high accuracy, even for the large dataset it runs efficiently and it can maintain accuracy when a large proportion of data is missing.
  • XGBoost is a popular and efficient open-source implementation of the gradient boosted trees algorithm. Gradient boosting is a supervised learning algorithm, which attempts to accurately predict a target variable by combining the estimates of a set of simpler, weaker models. Although XGBoost is one of the best algorithms for solving classification and regression problems, it is very sensitive to outliers since every classifier is forced to fix the errors in the predecessor learners.
  • Similarly to XGBoost, LightGBM is a distributed and efficient gradient boosting framework that uses tree-based learning for ranking, classification, and regression tasks. It is histogram-based and places continuous values into discrete bins, which leads to faster training and more efficient memory usage. LightGBM is much faster than XGBoost and it deals better with big datasets. However, Light GBM is sensitive to overfitting and thus can easily overfit small data.

4.2 Balancing data¶

As in our dataset we observe 16324 “0” and 64 “1” it means that our data is highly unbalanced.

The observed class of “0” are the majority class as they make up the largest proportion of the dataset, while the class of “1” is the minority class.

An effective way to handle imbalanced data is to downsample and upweight the majority class.

  • Downsampling means training on a disproportionately low subset of the majority class examples.

  • Upweighting means adding an example weight to the downsampled class equal to the factor by which you downsampled.

5. Conclusions¶

The main struggle of this project has been working with an extremely imbalanced dataset. The Logit Regression model implemented by Citi team was also having already quite good results, which made the range of improvement very narrow.

Eventhough and after a quite demanding understanding of their code and model, by a theoretical understanding of the variables, new ones were added to the original model and made a slight improvement on it.

In order to get an even bigger improvement 3 models were tested, with Random Forest Classifier, using Down-Sampling techinique to deal with the imbalance of the data, being the best one. A final Accuracy Ratio (AR) of 89% and an Area Under the ROC Curve (ROC_AUC) of 94% was reached, which is consider a quite good improvement.
